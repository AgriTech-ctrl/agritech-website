Farming Basics Guide

üå± How to Start

  • Find a sunny spot - plants need 6-8 hours of sun
  • Check your soil is good (not too acid, not too alkaline)
  • Start with easy plants like lettuce and carrots
  • Plan where to put each plant before you start

üåø Prepare the Soil

  • Remove all weeds and rubbish from your garden
  • Add compost or manure to make soil better
  • Dig the soil deep (about 20-30cm deep)
  • Make raised beds if water stays on the ground

üíß Water and Look After Plants

  • Water plants well but not too often (once or twice a week)
  • Water in the morning to keep plants healthy
  • Put mulch around plants to keep water in
  • Check plants often for bugs and sickness

üìÖ When to Plant

  • Plant at the right time for your area
  • Start seeds inside 6-8 weeks before winter ends
  • Plant new seeds every few weeks for more food
  • Write down what works in your garden

Water Saving Techniques

Learn how to conserve water while maintaining healthy crops and maximizing your farm's efficiency

üíß Smart Irrigation

  • Drip Irrigation: Water plants directly at the roots
  • Soaker Hoses: Slow, deep watering that prevents waste
  • Timed Systems: Automatic watering at optimal times
  • Rain Sensors: Skip watering when it's raining

üå± Soil Management

  • Mulching: Cover soil with organic matter to retain moisture
  • Compost: Add compost to improve water retention
  • Raised Beds: Better drainage and water control
  • Cover Crops: Plant crops that protect soil from drying

üïê Timing & Frequency

  • Early Morning: Water before 8 AM to reduce evaporation
  • Deep Watering: Water less often but more thoroughly
  • Weather Monitoring: Adjust watering based on weather
  • Plant Needs: Different plants need different amounts

‚ôªÔ∏è Water Recycling

  • Grey Water: Use household water for plants
  • Rainwater Harvesting: Collect and store rainwater
  • Water Storage: Use tanks and barrels for storage
  • Filtration: Clean water before using on plants

üåø Plant Selection

  • Drought-Resistant: Choose plants that need less water
  • Native Plants: Local plants adapt better to climate
  • Grouping: Plant similar water needs together
  • Seasonal Planning: Plant water-efficient crops in dry seasons

üìä Monitoring & Tools

  • Soil Moisture Meters: Check if plants need water
  • Water Usage Tracking: Monitor how much water you use
  • Weather Apps: Use technology to plan watering
  • Smart Controllers: Automated systems that save water
